Advanced Driver Assistance Systems

ADAS is an abbriviation for Advanced Driver Assistance Systems. ADAS is an evolution of older safety systems that are now standard features in most cars today such as ABS, Cruise Control, Parking Sensors and Automatic Lights. Mercedes Benz first introduced ABS in 1978 but it wasnâ€™t until 2004 that it became mandatory for a vehicles the be fitted with ABS.

Much the same will happen with ADAS. So far just a handful of vehicles are fitted with ADAS but as the years move on and technology advances, more and more of these Advanced Driver Assistance Systems will become standard equipment in cars.

Radar

Radar Sensors are usually used for pre-collision systems, high speed AEB systems blind spot monitors and adaptive cruise control systems.

Lidar

Lidar Sensors are often used for AEB systems in smaller city cars. More recently, Lidar is used to aid in monitoring the landscape arouns the vehicle.

Cameras

Cameras are essential for lane assist systems. In some veicles they are also used for pre-collision warnings and pedestrian and cyclist detection.
